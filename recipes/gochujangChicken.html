Gochujang Chicken

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup gochujang
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup apple juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated or minced
  • 1/2 inch piece fresh ginger, grated or minced
  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs chicken thighs

Wondering how to make this? Here's how.

  1. Make the paste:
    • Place the gochujang, maple syrup, apple juice, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a medium bowl or measuring cup.
    • Whisk to combine.
    • Place 1/2 cup of this paste in a small bowl and reserve the rest for basting while grilling.
  2. Brush the paste on the chicken:
    • Place chicken thighs in a large shallow pan.
    • Using a pastry brush or spoon, lightly brush both sides of the chicken with the paste.
    • Let the chicken sit at room temperature while you heat up the grill.
  3. Oil your grill grates and preheat your grill:
    • Using a paper towel and tongs, oil your clean grill grates with canola, vegetable, or another high heat oil.
    • Preheat one side of your gas grill to medium-high heat for 10-15 minutes, 400°F to 450°F, or push hot coals to one side of a charcoal grill.
    • The chicken will be transferred to the unheated side of the grill to finish cooking without direct heat.
  4. Grill the chicken:
    • Place chicken thighs, skin side down, on the oiled grates over the lit side of the grill.
    • Close cover and grill for 3 to 5 minutes, or until you see good grill marks form.
    • Flip the thighs and grill over direct heat again, an additional 3 to 5 minutes.
    • Move the chicken to the cool side of the grill, skin side up, brush with more paste, and close the lid.
  5. Baste the chicken and continue cooking:
    • After 10 minutes, brush the thighs with more paste. Using an instant-read thermometer, check the temperature of the chicken.
    • It should read is between 150°F to 155°F.
    • Close lid and continue cooking until the thighs reach 165°F to 170°F, 10 to 15 minutes more.
  6. Garnish and serve:
    • Garnish with chopped chives, sliced scallions, and toasted sesame seeds. Serve.
